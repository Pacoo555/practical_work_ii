Introduction to Fundamentals of Computer Engineering

FCE covers essential topics in computer engineering, beginning with the history of computer science and moving towards the latest advancements in artificial intelligence and big data. It starts by examining key historical milestones, including Alan Turing's contributions and the progression of computers through various generations. FCE also covers software development, focusing on Operating systems, methodologies, and programming languages. It also explores hardware architecture, including the computer's structure, memory, and bus organization. FCE also studies the evolution of computer networks, focusing on the Internet's architecture, protocols, and addressing systems. It also covers important concepts like data, information and numerical systems, such as binary. Finally, it addresses the legal and ethical issues in computer engineering and examines new trends such as artificial intelligence, machine learning, and the Internet of Things.

Learning outcomes of FCE

The learning outcomes of FCE focus on having an introduction to the world of computer engineering, firstly recognizing it as a degree and profession. Students will learn about hardware, software, operating systems, networks, and new trends in technology. They will learn about information, data, and big data, and learn about software development processes and web programming, including HTML and CSS.
